methods

interpreters

  • program logics (axiomatic semantics): hoare, djikstra and separation logic, forward and backward, predicate transformers, incorrectness logic; kleene algebras (with tests, with types, with products), automatons, state machines, regular expressions, parsers
  • structural semantics: small step, contexts, frames, zipper-differentiation;
  • natural semantics: fuel, big step semantics, definitional, monadic interpreters, denotational semantics;

compilers

  • correct compilation, program calculation, bisimulation, contextual equivalence, applicative equivalencec, logical relations, ‚Ä¶

intermediate representations

  • binders: de-brujin indices and levels, higher-order syntax, nominal logic, cofinite quantification, co-debrujin;
  • polyhedral, SSA, SSI, ‚Ä¶

effects

  • streams, monads, transformers, free and freer, interaction trees and arrows, super monads, effects, algebraic effects, handlers, capability-passing style and evidence translation;
  • monadic reflection and bang-notation;
  • behavioural types, typestates, global and local, binary and multiparty session types, process calculi and game semantics, multitier, tierless, and choreographic programming, information flow control and program partitioning;
  • linear and affine types;

confidence (proof > probability > test)

  • total, constructive logic, termination and productivity, tactics and dependent pattern matching;
  • extrinsic and intrinsic proofs;
  • SMT solver
  • property-based-testing, quickchick, probabilistic programming, automatic shrinking, lazy, exhaustive testing, ‚Ä¶; fuzzing

possible applications

  • safe multiparty computation - session types; confidentiality, integrity and availability
  • scalable world computer - smart contracts, state channels and second layer technology
  • fault tolerance - convergent data types & quorums & more
  • incremental computation - via mixed imperative & functional reactive programming
  • machine learning - differential & probalisitic programming
